## Run your Agents with OneContext
1. Click **Add Context** and enter a context name.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/1.1.png"></div>

2. Click **New Session** to create your first session with a working path.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/1.2.1.png"></div>
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/1.2.2.png"></div>

3. Start your work. The session summary will be shown automatically on the left.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/1.3.png"></div>

4. You can create multiple sessions under the same context by repeating steps 2–3.

5. A short context summary is provided. Hover over the context name to see the detailed version.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/1.5.png"></div>

6. Double-click the context name to view more details.


## Share Agent Context
1. Click **Share** to share your context. A message and a shareable link will be generated.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/2.1.1.png"></div>
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/2.1.2.png"></div>

2. Copy the link and share it with anyone, anywhere.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/2.2.png"></div>

3. You can also directly ask the context in Slack.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/2.3.png"></div>

## Load a Shared Context
1. If you receive a shared link, click it to open the context. You can ask questions about the received context directly in the chat.
   
2. You can also import the shared session locally.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/3.1.png"></div>

3. Return to OneContext, where you will find the imported session. You can continue working by creating new sessions under it.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/3.3.1.png"></div>
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/3.3.2.png"></div>

## Archieve a Session
1. Click **×** to close your terminal or session.
  
2. To resume the session:
a) Click **+**, then select **Resume Session**, or
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/4.2a.png"></div>

b) Double-click the context name (step6 in 'Run your Agents with OneContext').

3. Select the session you want to resume and click **Resume**.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/4.3.png"></div>

4. The session will reappear in its original position.

## Archieve a Context
1. Click **×** to archive the entire context.
  
2. To restore it, go to the **Config tab** and enable Show Archived Agents.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/5.2.png"></div>

3. Return to the **Contexts tab** to view all archived contexts.

4. Click **Restore Agent**. The context will be restored, and you can continue adding or resuming sessions, or share it again.
<div align="center"><img src="https://github.com/TheAgentContextLab/OneContext/blob/main/assets/5.4.png"></div>
